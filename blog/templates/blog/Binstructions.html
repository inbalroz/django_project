Instructions to participate in the bidding experiment


In this experiment, you are simulating a reviewer that needs to bid on papers to review.

You have a list of personal keywords that reflect your professional interests.

Your payment will be determined according to the  {{papers_get}} papers you are assigned and how well they match your personal keywords. The experiment only includes the bidding part. You will not need to actually read or review any paper.


Bidding

To bid on a paper, click on “Maybe" or “Yes".

You may click on “show abstract" to see the paper abstract.

You may also search the list (Ctrl-F) for your keywords or any other word.

You may bid on any number of papers. 

Important: you have to click “save" before moving to another page or tab (including the Instructions tab).


Assignment

When the experiment is over, you will be assigned exactly {{papers_get}} papers. 

The papers you will be assigned depend on your bid. That is, you are more likely to get a paper that you bid on. A “Yes" bid increases the chance more than a “Maybe" bid. 


Payment

You will get a payment of {{money}} NIS for your participation, plus a bonus that depends on your assignment.

Each of these {{papers_get}} assigned papers will affect your payment according to its relevance:

 -If the paper contains (in the title, keywords, or abstract) at least 2 of your personal keywords, then it is relevant for you, and worth {{extra_money_per_word_1}} NIS.

 -If it contains at least 3 words, then it is highly relevant, and worth {{extra_money_per_word_2}} NIS.

-If it contains no relevant words at all, it is irrelevant, and we will deduct {{deduct_money}}  NIS from your payment.


We request that you bid positively (yes/maybe) on at least {{paper_num_to_choose}} papers, but you do not have to follow this instruction and it will not affect your payment.  


Example

Jane has the personal keywords {NLP, vectors, multiprocessing, LDA, quantum computing, coil}.

Jane bids “Maybe" on papers {#34, #66, #193, #201}  

Jane bids “Yes" on papers {#12, #25, #174, #196, #304, #332, #347, #454, #466}


After the experiment is over, Jane is assigned 8 papers:

  {#174, #196, #304, #332, #347, #66, #3, #189}  

(that is, 5 papers with “Yes" bid, 1 paper with “Maybe" bid, and 2 papers with “No" bid).
